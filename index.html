About Me

Hello, I'm Naveen Mendola. I grew up in a small village in the mountains of Uttarakhand. During my Master's studies, I began exploring coupled oscillators and nonlinear dynamics as a structured subject, and it immediately captured my interest. My first research project focused on chimera states in Dr. Sarika Jalan's lab, which later became the core of my Master's thesis.

I was fortunate to work alongside amazing labmates, seniors, and mentors during this period. The collaborative environment, and how the group discussed recent research and their work further deepened my curiosity and inspired me to learn more tools and theory along the way.

Later, I joined Dr. Thounaojam at Bennett University for my PhD. He encouraged me to actively engage with the recent research in the field and to experiment freely using my programming skills. Through his guidance, I began working on ideas that intrigued me right away, many of which I might not have pursued otherwise. I could write some of them here in other projects. Please have a look.

Primary Research Interests

  • Computational neuroscience
  • Mathematical modeling of complex systems
  • Limit-cycle oscillators and synchronization
  • Nonlinear dynamics and chaos
